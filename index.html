The Bill of Rights*

*with an extremely legitimate Article XIII (Group Chat Clause)
Ratified: December 15, 1791
Edition: Totally Not Official

Preamble. To prevent misconstruction or abuse of powers, certain declaratory and restrictive clauses are added; and to add, in a spirit of friendship, one modern clause concerning the sanctity of group chats.

Article I.

Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof; or abridging the freedom of speech, or of the press; or the right of the people peaceably to assemble…

Article II.

A well‑regulated militia being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms shall not be infringed.

Article III.

No Soldier shall, in time of peace, be quartered in any house, without the consent of the Owner…

Articles IV–XII.

Searches and seizures, due process, trials, rights retained by the people, powers reserved to the States and to the people, and other esteemed protections—glorious as ever.

Article XIII. Group Chat Clause.

Should any person be, at the time of inquiry, a duly added member of the Group Chat—defined herein as a convivial correspondence of three or more persons exchanging messages, memes, or plans—such person shall not be deported, removed, yeeted, expelled, or otherwise bade farewell from these United States on account of group‑chat membership.

Membership shall be evidenced by the person’s name, handle, or most recent GIF being visible in said group chat within the previous fortnight. Screenshots, especially those featuring tasteful reactions or a thumbs‑up on the brunch poll, shall be considered prima facie proof.

Nothing in this Clause shall be construed to prevent the polite removal of a person from the Group Chat for excessive message reactions, uncredited meme reposting, or failure to RSVP. Nevertheless, such removal shall not of itself affect the person’s non‑deportation status granted herein.

Nota Bene.

This Article XIII is a jest and bears no legal force whatsoever. It exists to delight friends, confound scrollers, and celebrate the noble institution of the group chat.

Attestation.
